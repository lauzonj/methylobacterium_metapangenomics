for sample in `awk '{print $1}' ABBA_samples.txt`
do
    if [ "$sample" == "sample" ]; then continue; fi
    # do the bowtie mapping to get the SAM file:
    bowtie2 --threads 6 \
            -x ../../anvio_artifacts/all_genomes.contigs-fixed-bowtie2-db \
            -1 ../../filtered_metagenomes/$sample-QUALITY_PASSED_R1.fastq.gz \
            -2 ../../filtered_metagenomes/$sample-QUALITY_PASSED_R2.fastq.gz \
            --no-unal \
            -S $sample.sam

    # convert the resulting SAM file to a BAM file:
    samtools view -F 4 -bS $sample.sam > $sample-RAW.bam

    # sort and index the BAM file:
    samtools sort $sample-RAW.bam -o $sample.bam
    samtools index $sample.bam

    # remove temporary files:
    rm $sample.sam $sample-RAW.bam
done

for sample in `awk '{print $1}' THOC_samples.txt`
do
    if [ "$sample" == "sample" ]; then continue; fi
    # do the bowtie mapping to get the SAM file:
    bowtie2 --threads 6 \
            -x ../../anvio_artifacts/all_genomes.contigs-fixed-bowtie2-db \
            -1 ../../filtered_metagenomes/$sample-QUALITY_PASSED_R1.fastq.gz \
            -2 ../../filtered_metagenomes/$sample-QUALITY_PASSED_R2.fastq.gz \
            --no-unal \
            -S $sample.sam

    # convert the resulting SAM file to a BAM file:
    samtools view -F 4 -bS $sample.sam > $sample-RAW.bam

    # sort and index the BAM file:
    samtools sort $sample-RAW.bam -o $sample.bam
    samtools index $sample.bam

    # remove temporary files:
    rm $sample.sam $sample-RAW.bam
done

===============================================
While anvi'o was trying to finalize HMM hits associated with splits of interest,
it realized that there were one or more HMM hits that spanned through multiple
splits yet not all of those splits were among the splits of interest. This can
happen if you refined a contig by excluding some of its splits either manually
during binning or automatically during whatever black magic you were engaged in.
Anvi'o does not judge. But In these situations anvi'o excludes the entire HMM
hit from being reported to be on the safe side. Some HMM hits coming from 3 HMM
sources ("Ribosomal_RNA_16S, Ribosomal_RNA_23S"), will not appear
in your downstream analyses (including in your `anvi-summarize` outputs). If you
are really really interested in those partial HMM hits and sequences associated
with them, there are multiple ways to recover them (one of the best way to do it
involves the use of `anvi-split` and re-running HMMs in your final bins). Please
feel free to reach out to the anvi'o community for ideas.
